Here’s How To Get Free Flu, COVID Vaccines In Chicago This Fall

The city’s health department is working with aldermanic offices to host vaccination clinics citywide, providing shots for free.
by Pavan Acharya October 4, 2024October 4, 2024

CHICAGO The city is hosting vaccination clinics for people looking to get free COVID-19 or flu shots for the season.

Chicago residents older than 6 months will be able to get free vaccines at ward offices throughout the city through at least late November. The vaccines do not require insurance and have no associated out-of-pocket costs, according to the Chicago Department of Public Health’s website.

Most alderpeople are working with the health department to host the vaccination clinics, which last three to four hours.

Though pre-registration for vaccines through a city website is recommended, the clinics will also allow walk-ins, a health department spokesperson said.
